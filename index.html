Syllabus Overview

This section introduces the underlying ideas and concepts of business and includes the purpose and nature of business activity and how businesses can be classified.

1.1 Business activity

  • Concepts of needs, wants, scarcity and opportunity cost
  • Importance of specialisation
  • Purpose of business activity
  • The concept of adding value and how added value can be increased

What is the main purpose of business activity?

The main purpose of business activity is to satisfy customers' needs and wants while making a profit.

What are two non-financial methods of motivation?

Two non-financial methods of motivation are job enrichment and recognition/praise.

  • The role of marketing
  • Market changes

What is market segmentation?

Market segmentation is the process of dividing a market into distinct groups of consumers with different needs, characteristics, or behaviors.

4.1 Production of goods and services

  • The meaning of production

What are the three main methods of production?

The three main methods of production are job production, batch production, and flow production.

This section covers the need for and sources of business finance, cash-flow forecasting and working capital.

5.1 Business finance: needs and sources

  • The need for business finance
  • The main sources of capital
  • Cash flow forecasting

What is working capital?

Working capital is the capital available for conducting the day-to-day operations of the business, calculated as current assets minus current liabilities.

This section focuses on different external influences on business activity and how these impact on a business.

6.1 Economic influences

  • Business cycle
  • Interest rates
  • Exchange rates
  • Inflation

How might high interest rates affect businesses?

High interest rates can increase borrowing costs for businesses, reduce consumer spending, and potentially slow down economic growth.
